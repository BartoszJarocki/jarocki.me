# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary reader is Bartosz himself — this is a public notebook for his work, writing, and references. Fellow engineers and lurkers passing through (often via a note, an OG image on social, or a search hit) are the secondary audience. No recruiters-first framing, no contact-form funnel: visitors come to read, browse, and form an impression of the person, not to convert.

Reader posture: exploring, not deciding. Someone who landed on a note about side projects might wander to /uses, then /about, then back to a note from 2022. The site should reward that wandering.

## Product Purpose

Identity and craft showcase. The site itself is the artifact — proof of taste and engineering practice, not a marketing surface for them. Success is when a reader closes a tab and the impression that lingers is "this person makes things carefully," without that being said anywhere on the page.

Functional core: a Notion-backed notes system, a current-and-past work record, a /uses page, a /creating list of side projects. None of those need to be loud; all of them need to be exact.

## Brand Personality

Considered, dry, candid.

Voice already audible in the codebase: self-aware lines in /uses ("If I'm going to slouch in the worst ergonomic position imaginable all day, I might as well do it in an expensive chair"), a Stoic quotes section taken seriously, an /about that's a real bio rather than a pitch. Keep that voice. No marketing enthusiasm, no growth-hacker copy, no "Trusted by" energy. Quiet confidence — the engineer's version, not the designer's.

Emotional register: calm, slightly amused, never striving.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Next.js + Tailwind dev portfolio.** Hero with avatar, three project cards, a CTA. The Spotlight-template lineage this site started from. The whole point of evolving is to stop reading as one of these.
- **Corporate SaaS landing.** Gradient hero, feature-card grid, logo wall, testimonial slider, "trusted by" anything. Wrong register entirely.
- **Dribbble showpiece.** Motion-heavy, over-designed, form-over-substance. Demos that exist to impress the designer audience rather than serve the reader. Avoid the impulse to overcompensate against template-feel by sliding into this.
- **Magazine-grade essayist sites** (Craig Mod, etc.) are admired but not the target. The site is an engineer's notebook, not a literary one.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the proof.** Claims about craft are made by the page, not on it. If a reader can't sense care from the typography, spacing, and detail, no amount of "passionate software engineer" copy will recover it.
2. **Public notebook, not portfolio.** Reward browsing. The home page is a starting point, not a funnel. Cross-links between notes, work, and references should feel inviting, never optimized.
3. **Distinct, not loud.** Move out of the Spotlight-template silhouette by sharpening, not by adding. New layouts, real typographic hierarchy, considered color use — not extra motion or decoration.
4. **Engineer's confidence, not designer's flash.** Precision in type, rhythm, and detail beats animation reels and visual tricks. References are rauno.me / paco.me / leerob.io: craftsperson sites where the restraint is the statement.
5. **Every word and every pixel earns its place.** Cut restated headings, decorative dividers, filler copy, gratuitous icons. The voice is dry; the layout should match.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline. Real focus states, keyboard navigation through all interactive elements, sufficient contrast across both light and dark themes, alt text on meaningful images, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected wherever motion is introduced. Nothing exotic, nothing skipped.
